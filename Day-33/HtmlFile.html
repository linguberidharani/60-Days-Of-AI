Headline Detective

The concept: headline-article gap

Publishers know that a headline's only job is to earn a click — the article's job is to be accurate. When money and attention are on the line, those two goals can pull apart. Words like "SHOCKING," "destroys," or "secretly" are chosen for adrenaline, not precision.

Why it matters: most people only ever read the headline. If the headline overstates the story, that overstatement becomes what you "know" — even if the article itself was careful.

Emotion Detector

The concept: emotion drives sharing

Content that makes you feel fear, outrage, guilt, or urgency gets shared far more than neutral content — because strong emotion short-circuits the "should I check this first?" instinct. Creators and algorithms both learn this fast.

Why it matters: the feeling a post gives you often isn't a side effect — it's the point. Recognizing the technique doesn't mean the topic is fake, but it does mean you should pause before reacting or resharing.
