Nutrition Sources & Methods
Where the data comes from and how calculations are performed.
⚠️ Educational Tool — Not Medical Advice
NutriScope is a personal nutrition education tool. All nutritional data, targets, and recommendations are estimates based on general population guidelines. Individual needs vary based on health conditions, medications, genetics, and other factors. Data should not be used to diagnose, treat, or manage any medical condition. Always consult a registered dietitian (RD) or licensed physician for personalised nutrition guidance.
Food Composition Data
🇮🇳
ICMR-NIN (Indian Council of Medical Research – National Institute of Nutrition)
Nutritional values for Indian foods (Rice, Roti, Dal, Paneer, Chana, Rajma, Poha, Idli, Dosa, etc.) are derived from the NIN Food Composition Tables, a gold-standard reference for South Asian foods published in Hyderabad, India.
🇺🇸
USDA FoodData Central (fdc.nal.usda.gov)
Used for globally common foods including Oats, Bread, Egg, Chicken, Fish, Banana, Apple, Milk, Potato, Spinach, and many of the 40 extended foods. Values are per 100g edible portion unless noted.
🌐
WHO / FAO Codex Alimentarius
Cross-referenced for micronutrient data (iron, calcium, zinc, folate, vitamins) especially for plant-based and fortified foods.
Calculation Methods
🔢
Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) — Mifflin-St Jeor Equation (1990)
Male: BMR = 10×W + 6.25×H − 5×A + 5 | Female: BMR = 10×W + 6.25×H − 5×A − 161. W=weight(kg), H=height(cm), A=age(yrs). This equation has ±10% accuracy vs indirect calorimetry in healthy adults.
⚡
Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE)
TDEE = BMR × Physical Activity Level (PAL). PAL factors: Sedentary 1.2, Light 1.375, Moderate 1.55, Very Active 1.725, Athlete 1.9. Source: FAO/WHO/UNU (2004) Human Energy Requirements.
🥩
Macronutrient Targets
Protein: 15% of TDEE (÷4 kcal/g) | Carbohydrates: 55% of TDEE (÷4 kcal/g) | Fat: 30% of TDEE (÷9 kcal/g). Based on ICMR-NIN RDA (2020) and WHO dietary guidelines.
🧬
Micronutrient RDA Sources
Iron, Calcium, Zinc, Folate, Vitamin A/C/D/B12, Magnesium, Potassium, Omega-3 targets are based on ICMR-NIN Recommended Dietary Allowances for Indians (2020), with age/gender stratification applied to the 19-50 yrs adult range used in this tool.
Limitations to Know
⚠️
Bioavailability is not modelled. For example, non-heme iron from plant sources (Dal, Spinach) is absorbed at ~5–10%, vs heme iron from meat at ~20–30%. This tool shows total intake, not absorbed amounts.
⚠️
Cooking losses are not applied. Actual nutrient content changes with heat, water, and preparation method. Raw food values are used throughout.
⚠️
Individual variation is significant. Gut microbiome, genetics (e.g. MTHFR for folate), medications, and health conditions can dramatically alter actual nutritional needs and absorption.
⚠️
Pregnancy, breastfeeding, and disease states require significantly different targets not covered here. Consult an ICMR-trained RD for such cases.
