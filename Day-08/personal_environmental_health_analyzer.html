Executive summary

Analysis of 9 major Indian cities (June 2026) confirms New Delhi as the most polluted (AQI 113) while Mumbai leads as cleanest (AQI 45). Visakhapatnam sits at AQI 89 — in the Moderate band but better than Delhi, Kolkata, Chennai and Bangalore, primarily because Eastern Ghats + Bay of Bengal breezes continuously flush pollutants seaward. The RINL steel plant (7.3 MTPA) causes PM10 spikes 30–50% above city average in Gajuwaka. Coastal aquifer TDS ranges 380–800 mg/L with moderate hardness, elevating hair and skin risk. Overall Vizag scores 62/100 — moderate, better than most Indian metros but still far below WHO safe thresholds.

Most surprising observation

Despite hosting one of India's largest steel plants (RINL, 7.3 MTPA), Visakhapatnam's AQI (89) is better than Chennai, Bangalore, and Kolkata. The Eastern Ghats act as a windbreak while Bay of Bengal sea breezes provide continuous natural air scrubbing — a geographic advantage no inland city can replicate. Vizag's PM2.5 is 50–70% lower than Delhi's.
